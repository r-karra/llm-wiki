# English Tenses Reference

**Summary**: A comprehensive guide and quick-reference table for the 12 English tenses and conditional structures, detailing their usage, formulas, and examples.

**Resource**: [Tenses Table PDF](../../raw/tenses_table.pdf)

---

This reference page provides a detailed breakdown of English tenses, based on the `tenses_table.pdf` resource. Understanding the mechanics of tenses is essential for natural flow and grammatical accuracy in professional American English.

## The Tenses Table

| Tense | Use / Function | Form / Formula | Examples (Affirmative / Negative / Interrogative) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Simple Present** | • Repeated actions / habits<br>• General truths / facts<br>• Timetables / scheduled future events | `infinitive` / `infinitive + s` (he/she/it) | • I work. / I don't work. / Do I work?<br>• He works. / He doesn't work. / Does he work? |
| **Present Progressive** | • Action happening now / around now<br>• Fixed future plans / arrangements | `be (am/are/is) + verb-ing` | • I'm working. / I'm not working. / Am I working?<br>• He's working. / He isn't working. / Is he working? |
| **Simple Past** | • Action completed in the past at a specific time | `regular: infinitive + ed`<br>`irregular: 2nd form` | • I worked. / I didn't work. / Did I work?<br>• I went. / I didn't go. / Did I go? |
| **Past Progressive** | • Action in progress in the middle of another past action / specific time | `was/were + verb-ing` | • I was working. / I wasn't working. / Was I working?<br>• He was working. / He wasn't working. / Was he working? |
| **Simple Present Perfect** | • Finished action with connection to present<br>• Action started in past, continuing now | `have/has + past participle` (3rd form) | • I have worked. / I haven't worked. / Have I worked?<br>• He has worked. / He hasn't worked. / Has he worked? |
| **Present Perfect Progressive** | • Action began in past and just stopped / is continuing<br>• Focuses on duration of the action | `have/has + been + verb-ing` | • I have been working. / I haven't been working. / Have I been working?<br>• He has been working. / He hasn't been working. / Has he been working? |
| **Simple Past Perfect** | • Action completed before another action in the past | `had + past participle` | • I had worked. / I hadn't worked. / Had I worked?<br>• He had worked. / He hadn't worked. / Had he worked? |
| **Past Perfect Progressive** | • Duration of an action up to a certain point in the past | `had + been + verb-ing` | • I had been working. / I hadn't been working. / Had I been working?<br>• He had been working. / He hadn't been working. / Had he been working? |
| **will - future** | • Predictions (what you think will happen)<br>• Spontaneous decisions / offers<br>• Main clause in conditional type I sentences | `will + infinitive` | • I'll work. / I won't work. / Will I work?<br>• He'll work. / He won't work. / Will he work? |
| **going to - future** | • Prior plans / intentions for the future<br>• Logical predictions based on evidence | `be (am/are/is) + going to + infinitive` | • I'm going to work. / I'm not going to work. / Am I going to work?<br>• He's going to work. / He's not going to work. / Is he going to work? |
| **Future Progressive** | • Action in progress at a specific time in the future | `will + be + verb-ing` | • I'll be working. / I won't be working. / Will I be working?<br>• He'll be working. / He won't be working. / Will he be working? |
| **Future Perfect Simple** | • Action that will be completed before a specific point in the future | `will + have + past participle` | • I'll have worked. / I won't have worked. / Will I have worked?<br>• He'll have worked. / He won't have worked. / Will he have worked? |
| **Future Perfect Progressive** | • Duration of action up to a certain point in the future | `will + have + been + verb-ing` | • I'll have been working. / I won't have been working. / Will I have been working?<br>• He'll have been working. / He won't have been working. / Will he have been working? |
| **Conditional Simple** | • Action that might happen<br>• Main clause in conditional type II sentences | `would + infinitive` | • I would work. / I wouldn't work. / Would I work?<br>• He would work. / He wouldn't work. / Would he work? |
| **Conditional Progressive** | • Action that might be in progress<br>• Focuses on hypothetical duration | `would + be + verb-ing` | • I would be working. / I wouldn't be working. / Would I be working?<br>• He would be working. / He wouldn't be working. / Would he be working? |
| **Conditional Perfect** | • Action that might have happened in the past<br>• Main clause in conditional type III sentences | `would + have + past participle` | • I would have worked. / I wouldn't have worked. / Would I have worked?<br>• He would have worked. / He wouldn't have worked. / Would he have worked? |
| **Conditional Perfect Progressive** | • Action that might have been in progress in the past | `would + have + been + verb-ing` | • I would have been working. / I wouldn't have been working. / Would I have been working?<br>• He would have been working. / He wouldn't have been working. / Would he have been working? |

## Signal Words Guide

- **Simple Present**: *every day, sometimes, always, often, usually, seldom, never, first... then*
- **Present Progressive**: *now, at the moment, Look!, Listen!*
- **Simple Past**: *last..., ... ago, in 1990, yesterday*
- **Past Progressive**: *while*
- **Simple Present Perfect**: *just, yet, never, ever, already, so far, up to now, since, for, recently*
- **Present Perfect Progressive**: *all day, the whole day, how long, since, for*
- **Simple Past Perfect**: *already, just, never*
- **Past Perfect Progressive**: *how long, since, for*

## Accent and Stress Integration

When practicing these tenses in American English, pay attention to:
1. **Contractions**: Contractions are vital in spoken American English (e.g. *I'll*, *I'm*, *won't*, *wasn't*, *hadn't*, *wouldn't*).
2. **Schwa sound**: Auxiliary verbs like *been*, *have*, *has*, *was*, *were* are often unstressed and reduce to the schwa ($\partial$) in natural speech.
