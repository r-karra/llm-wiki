# English Grammar: Questions

**Summary**: A comprehensive guide to forming questions in English, covering word order, auxiliary verbs, passive voice, and question tags.

---

## 1. Basic Question Formation (Word Order)

In English, questions often involve a change in word order (inversion) or the use of auxiliary verbs.

### Questions with the Verb 'be'
Subject and verb change positions.

| Sentence Type | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Statement** | Subject + be + ... | You are from Germany. |
| **Question** | Be + Subject + ...? | Are you from Germany? |

### Wh-Questions with 'be'
| Question Word | Verb | Subject | Complement | Answer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Where | are | you | from? | I am from Stuttgart. |
| What | is | your name | | My name is Peter. |
| How | are | Pat and Sue | | They are fine. |

---

## 2. Questions with 'have' and 'have got'

### have as a Main Verb (American English)
Requires the auxiliary `do/does`.
- **Do** you **have** a cat? $\rightarrow$ Yes, I **do**.
- **Does** your brother **have** a bike? $\rightarrow$ Yes, he **does**.

### have as an Auxiliary (British English: 'have got')
- **Have** you **got** a cat? $\rightarrow$ Yes, I **have**.
- **Has** your brother **got** a bike? $\rightarrow$ Yes, he **has**.

---

## 3. Simple Present and Simple Past Questions

### Simple Present (do/does)
| Question Word | Auxiliary | Subject | Verb | Complement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| | Do | you | read | books? |
| What | do | you | play | on your computer? |
| When | does | your mother | go | to work? |

### Simple Past (did)
| Question Word | Auxiliary | Subject | Verb | Complement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| | Did | Max | play | football? |
| What | did | you | play | yesterday evening? |

**Note**: For the verb **be** in the past, use `was/were` without `did`.
- **Were** you in Leipzig last week? (NOT: Did you were...)

---

## 4. Subject vs. Object Questions

| Type | Focus | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Subject Question** | The person/thing doing the action | Question Word + Verb + ... | Who **runs** the shop? (Peter runs it.) |
| **Object Question** | The person/thing receiving the action | Q-Word + Auxiliary + Subject + Verb | Who **did** Mandy **phone**? (She phoned her uncle.) |

---

## 5. Questions in the Passive Voice

Passive questions are formed with `be` + **past participle**.

### Simple Present Passive
- **Is** the test **written** in room 311?
- **Are** grapes **grown** in California?

### Simple Past Passive
- **Was** the book **taken** to the classroom?
- **Why were** the students **taught** at home?

### Other Tenses (Passive)
| Tense | Structure |
|---|---|
| **Present Perfect** | Has the house **been built**? |
| **will-future** | Will the house **be built**? |
| **Present Progressive** | Is the house **being built**? |

---

## 6. Modal Verbs: Questions with 'can'

`Can` remains the same regardless of the subject.
- **Can** you speak English? $\rightarrow$ Yes, I can.
- **What can** they ride? $\rightarrow$ They can ride skateboards.

---

## 7. Short Answers

In English, it is more polite to use short answers rather than a simple "Yes" or "No".

| Question | Short Answer |
|---|---|
| Is he from London? | Yes, he is. / No, he isn't. |
| Do they live in a flat? | Yes, they do. / No, they don't. |
| Have you been to Scotland? | Yes, I have. / No, I haven't. |
| Will we arrive on time? | Yes, we will. / No, we won't. |

---

## 8. Special Question Types

### Which vs. What
- **Which**: Used when there is a **limited choice** (e.g., "Which lemonade do you like – green or pink?").
- **What**: Used when there is an **unlimited choice** (e.g., "What lemonade do you like?").

### Question Tags
Used in spoken English to ask for agreement or confirmation.
- **Positive statement $\rightarrow$ Negative tag**: You are Tom, **aren't you**?
- **Negative statement $\rightarrow$ Positive tag**: He isn't Joe, **is he**?
- **Let's**: Let's take the bus, **shall we**?
- **Imperatives**: Open the window, **will you**? / **won't you**?
